Tribute to Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

"The Missile Man of India" — Former President, Scientist & Teacher


Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam (1931 - 2015)

Early Life

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He came from a humble background and worked hard to become one of India's greatest scientists.

Education & Career

  • Studied physics and aerospace engineering.
  • Worked at DRDO and ISRO — instrumental in India's missile and space programs.
  • Played a leading role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
  • Served as the 11th President of India (2002–2007).

Major Achievements

Year Achievement
1980s–1990s Led development of ballistic missiles (Agni, Prithvi)
1998 Advised on Pokhran-II nuclear tests
2002 Became President of India
2008–2015 Authored books, inspired students nationwide

Quotes & Philosophy

"Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."
"If you want to shine like a sun, first burn like a sun."

Timeline
